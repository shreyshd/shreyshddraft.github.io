Learning How You Feel



The first step of learning how to feel is educating yourself about mental illnesses. Before you read this page, take a deep breath and ground yourself. Think of a calming scene in your mind. Now, let's get started!




Depression:

Depression is a type of psychological illness characterized by a continuous sense of dread and a decreased interest in doing any activity. Individuals with depression undergo emotional distress, or helplessness, as well as the feeling of being exhausted all the time, which causes them to isolate themselves from members of their family as well as friends.

Symptoms: fear or sadness, low self esteem, extreme mood swings, low energy, and sleeping issues

Eating Disorders:

Eating disorders are behavioral emotions that cause eating routines to be disrupted. Individuals with eating disorders have terrible composure and self-control, which might lead to future problems within relationships.

Symptoms: excessive restriction, excessive binging, constantly losing or gaining weight, linking a self esteem with one's diet.

Bipolar Depression:

Bipolar depression is a mental illness characterized by periods of sorrow followed by periods of extreme exhilaration. Bipolar disorder is a very subjective experience, and nobody has the same perspective as the other. This disorder can cause mood swings that can last anywhere from a few days to many months, and they might be linked to suicide tendencies.

Symptoms: Mood swings, anxiety, depression, OCD, guilt, hopelessness, a loss of interest in activities that were once fun, suicidal thoughts.

OCD:

Obsessive-Obsessive Disorder is marked by irrational concerns and ideas that contribute to irresistible urges. The obsessions from this disorder are disturbing, unwelcome sensations, desires, or ideas that are repetitive, continuous, and create anguish or worry.

Symptoms: The repetitive fears and behavior that causes him/her to organize items in a certain way or act repetitively in a certain way. Ex: Washing your hands exactly 10 times before eating every meal.
